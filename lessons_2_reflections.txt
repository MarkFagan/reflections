What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?
Creates a .git file that identifies files in current dir as untracked

How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository? What value do you think it offers?
Staging Area may hold several related files to a commit.  

How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change?
Using git status you can see how many files are in the staging area that would be associated with a commit.

What are some situations when branches would be helpful in keeping your history organized? How would branches help?
Branches help to try out new features or changes that you may not want on the master branch.  This is especially important when working in a collabortive developement environment.

How do the diagrams help you visualize the branch structure?
See where commits and braches are reachable

What is the result of merging two branches together? Why do we represent it in the diagram the way we do?
Merging adds new features or changes to a common branch.  Diagram shows how the branches relate to one another.

What are the pros and cons of Git’s automatic merging vs. always doing merges manually?
Pros it makes the merging process easier the cons is there may be conflicts or additional changes that you may not be aware of.
